Module 8: Research Methods and Professional Practice

A 12-Unit Module that Started on June 2023 and was Covered in 12 Weeks by Karen Outram

Summary

This module provided a practical foundation in research methods for conducting research in real-world settings.

It covered various tools and techniques, offers feedback through cases and project tasks, and addresses legal, social, ethical, and professional issues in computing projects.

Core textbooks explained research principles and their application in different organizations and continuous assessments included case studies and milestone assessments, promoting peer-to-peer and instructor interactions.

Creation of an e-portfolio was also crucial so as to demonstrate my abilities and reflections throughout this module.

Skills Acquired during this Module

  1. Ability to to assess professional, legal, ethical, and cultural issues in computing.

  2. Ability to apply academic investigation principles to a computing research topic, critically evaluate existing literature and research methods, and produce a well-considered research proposal.

  3. Developed research competencies, including data collection and analysis, and encourages a reflective and independent learning approach.

  4. Gained Knowledge on research ethics, scientific method, deductive and inductive reasoning, professionalism.

  5. Ability to assess validity and reliability, planning, critical analysis, extract insights from raw data and translating into meaningful insights, hypothesis testing, navigate datasets, summarize information and statistical proficiency.

  6. Awareness and understanding of qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods approaches.Including method selection, creativity, critical thinking and proficiency in research methods.

  7. Gained skills and knowledge on project management, risk mitigation and management, research writing, effective communication of findings. and creation of focused and criticala research proposals.
